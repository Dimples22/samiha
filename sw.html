The Option of Assisted Suicide

Published Mar 16, 2026

If medically assisted suicide were legal, would I choose it? I cannot say. Perhaps. But the question itself may not be the most important part.

What matters, I think, is simply knowing that the option exists.

There is a certain relief in imagining an exit. Not because I long for it, but because it means I would not be trapped. The thought of suffering without end can feel oppressive, like being shut in a room with no door. If a door exists, even one I may never open, the room is different.

Paradoxically, the possibility of leaving may make it easier to stay. If life is something I can still refuse, then continuing to live becomes a choice rather than a duty. Each day is no longer something imposed on me, but something I accept again.

In that sense, the exit matters less as a path to take than as a quiet reassurance in the background. Not an end I seek, but a reminder that I am free.

Perhaps I would never use such an option. Perhaps the knowledge alone would be enough. Sometimes relief lies not in the act itself, but in knowing that even the worst of it would not be endless.
