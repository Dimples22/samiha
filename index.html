About Me

It’s hard to sum up who I am in one paragraph. My book is about my life and what has made me who I am, yet I am someone who is constantly evolving, constantly experiencing. I wrote The Simulacrum because it felt like a long time coming. Writing is the one way I can take the time to articulate my thoughts, and this book is everything I’ve been keeping inside.

I believed that if no one could understand me, then maybe this could help them. Most people will never know what it is like to have a complicated childhood that seeps into the smallest parts of your day-to-day life. They might never stop to think that perhaps the little girl who was mute in school was mute for a reason. I hope my book can offer comfort to those who understand these feelings and awareness to those who may not so they might see a little more, think a little deeper, and be a little more mindful.

Why Work With Me

I put in the work instead of taking the easy way because I trust myself and know I can do better. I am almost a jack of all trades, interested in writing, drawing, coding, leading, content creation, designing, and more, and I am adaptable and learn fast. I am more passionate than most, and when I want to do something, I do it instead of procrastinating. I am independent and use my free time to grow my skills and knowledge, always pushing myself to improve and take on new challenges.

My Book

The Simulacrum

Blending fiction with lived experience, The Simulacrum belongs to the genre of autofiction, a form that blurs the boundaries between autobiography and imagination. Rather than following a traditional plot, it turns inward, tracing the protagonist’s inner world: her disintegration, her tentative reconstruction, and her philosophical reckonings with selfhood, love, and loss. Episodic, confessional, and deeply introspective, this book lingers on questions rather than answers.
