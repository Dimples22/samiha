The Seasons You Never Saw

Published Mar 07, 2026

You knew me as winter, but never the other seasons. Around you, I was the cold yet sheltered version of myself—the one you could turn to in struggle, the one that offered seldom comfort. You never saw my spring, growing and inquisitive, bursting with curiosity and the desire to try new things. You never glimpsed my summer, when I am luminous and unrestrained, laughing carelessly, letting warmth spill freely into the space around me. Nor did you witness my autumn, amber-hued and contemplative; the season in which I gather strength for the coming storm, cast off what I once was, and welcome winter’s hibernation.

I allowed only winter to exist around you; it felt inevitable, because the nature of our relationship and the limits of our conversations never extended beyond it. In that sense, you never truly knew me.

You delight in the company of others. In my world, however, yours is the only company I can tolerate. The word “tolerate” carries the sound of indifference, yet that is not quite what I mean. I could live my life without human connection. Still, when we speak, when our paths cross, there is something quietly pleasant in it.

You were the sun. Once, your warmth reached me, and I basked in it. But the sun shines on many faces as the earth turns, and not every face matters equally; in some regions, it lingers longer, shining more fully while moving swiftly past others. As selfish as it may sound, I cannot wait any longer for it to return to me.

Simply put, we are not compatible. You belong among people who move through the world as you do, who find meaning in closeness and easy conversation.

I will not miss you.

But I will remember you fondly for the time in my life when you mattered.
